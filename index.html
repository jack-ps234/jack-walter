KJ Jazz

Week 2 (4/26 - 5/3)

Welcome back to KJ Jazz! Due to finals and time constraints, we’ve decided to switch things up with just one song this week, but we promise to not disappoint. Really, I think the soul flowing from this song does the heavy lifting, we’re just glad to commentate on our experience with it. Packing a punch this week is the title track “Anatomy of Angels” by Jon Batiste

Deep Dive

  • This track seems to glide through many distinct journeys and weave them together so well. The energy rushes forward slowly aided by smooth play of the keys, and especially the saxophone. Despite how different each few minutes of the song sound, each section is connected so well.
  • Even though this is only one song in an album, it truly takes us on the full journey an album would. We start off with what feels like a slower balad, we move through an interlude and right into a more upbeat track then to a conceptual track. Throughout the 12 minute track, it takes us on a ride similar to that of an album, and without feeling rushed!
  • The meshing of the instruments is truly amazing during this song. To my ear, the saxophone initially stands out, but as you listen harder, the piano almost sets the path for the sax to follow, and the percussion gives such a vibrance and life to this song that it feels so soulful and raw.
  • Bonus: This track is part of an entire album… and the whole thing is live!

Sneaky Moments

Since we focused on a single song, we decided to choose moments by time stamp that kept us coming back!

  • J’s pick: 1:10 - 2:45. There are like three moments within this time frame where we get a beautiful slide from the saxophone (1:45, 2:25, and 2:35) and maybe it feels so good because it brings the resolution, the sound your ear is wanting to hear, at that precise moment. The piano, the bass, the drums, and the silence all bring about this “wait” that draws you into hearing the exact sliding resolution you are looking for. Not to mention some other excellent components on each instrument as the song passes through this section into something closer to the climax.
  • K’s pick: 2:55-5:45. This whole score feels like it is just conveying a positive outlook on life, the crisp trumpet with such a warm, clear tone just creates such an inviting section of this song that retains my attention long after the song ends. We can hear the saxophone waiting to take over, lulling in and out before fully taking over. The transition from trumpet to saxophone furthers this idea of community and connection, that feels almost like seeing an old friend in a cozy coffee shop.

KJ Jazz

Week 1 (4/19 - 4/26)

Hello and welcome to the first KJ Jazz album of the week! We are starting this brand new series off strong with the self-titled collaboration album, “Duke Ellington and John Coltrane.”

Deep Dive

  • At some points, I began to feel like this sounded like many other jazz albums I have heard, but what I really see is that these men are expressing the feelings and problems they had at the time, and our problems are not entirely unique or new. We are battling old problems and not battling them alone. This era is just expressing those.
  • With one of the strongest starts to a jazz album of all time, the track “In A Sentimental Mood” sets the tone for the whole album, creating a soft and compelling feeling, while leaving space for both Coltrane and Ellington to speak to their individual strengths.
  • The album is scattered with Coltrane’s fluttery saxophone licks, that feel light and energetic, that carry you through the album. Coltrane also does a wonderful job balancing these with his solos, which often feel like an expression of raw, unfiltered emotions coming across

Sneaky Picks

This section highlights the tracks we loved; ones that might fly under the radar, but absolutely deserve your attention.

  • J’s pick: “The feeling of jazz.” Each lick ended so sweetly and really leaves a beautiful picture of what jazz is.
  • K’s pick: “My Little Brown Book.” Following several higher energy tracks, this song gives us time to slow down and reflect, while encapsulating the feeling of smooth jazz perfectly
